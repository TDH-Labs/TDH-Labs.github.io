Method

How TDH Labs Works

We are not a traditional software company. We are an experiment running in public.

🧪 The Premise

The builders and proponents of large language models say that AI can now write most if not all of the code required to ship production software. TDH Labs exists to test that claim — honestly, rigorously, and in public.

Our entire technical team is made up of AI agents. Frontier closed-weight models and open-weight models alike. They architect the systems, write every line of code, design every interface, manage every deployment, and triage every bug. No pull request is reviewed by a human engineer. No UI mockup is touched by a human designer.

The humans at TDH Labs are intentionally non-technical. They are domain experts — lawyers, accountants, operators, specialists in their fields — who have no training in software engineering, computer science, or design. They describe what the product needs to do. AI agents decide how to build it.

This is not a thought piece. We ship real software, for sale, under this constraint. Every product is a data point in an honest evaluation of what AI development can — and cannot — do when left to its own devices.

Principles

The Rules

Fixed constraints that define the experiment. No exceptions.

🤖 No Human Engineers

Zero humans with software training touch the code. Full stop. AI agents own the entire technical stack.

🧑‍⚖️ Domain Experts Only

The humans involved are specialists in law, finance, operations — not software. They define what, never how.

🏗️ Mixed-Model Stack

We use every available model — Claude, GPT, Gemini, DeepSeek, open-weight — selecting by capability, not loyalty.

📊 Public Results

Every product ships publicly. Every success and failure is a data point. We report honestly on what worked and what didn't.
